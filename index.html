U.S. Climate Futures

Explore how temperature and precipitation across the United States change under different emissions scenarios through 2100.

Colors show change relative to each state's 1995–2014 average, not absolute temperature.

Project Progress

So far, we have built an interactive choropleth map of the United States that visualizes climate data from the NOAA CMIP6 dataset. The map displays state-level temperature anomalies and precipitation values sourced from the CESM2 climate model, spanning from 1950 to 2100 only covering the SSP5-8.5 (worst case) emissions scenario for now. Users can explore how climate conditions change year by year using the slider, toggle between temperature and precipitation views, and click on any state to see detailed statistics including average temperature in Fahrenheit and warming since the 1995–2014 baseline.

The most challenging aspect of this project will be implementing the full suite of interactive features planned for the final version, particularly a comparison function and a focus mode, as well as implementing the other SSPs. Designing intuitive UI for switching between three distinct map modes (full US view, single state focus, and side-by-side comparison) while keeping all controls functional and the experience feeling seamless will require careful planning. Additionally, integrating a scrollytelling narrative introduction that guides the reader through the climate story before handing them control of the map will be a significant design challenge.
